### Version 0.1 — Seed Layer

## I. The Core Philosophy

The ratio lattice is not a to-do list. It is not a priority matrix. It is not a productivity system.

It is a **declared scoring engine** that tells you what matters most *relative to everything else* — in real time, based on real signals from your actual life.

The foundational rule: **nothing has absolute value. Everything has comparative value relative to a declared basis.**

A task is not "important." A task is more important than these three other tasks, given this scoring profile, given the signals from the last 48 hours, given your current phase of work and life. Remove the comparator and the ranking collapses. Change the comparator and the whole queue reshuffles. This is not a flaw — it is the point.

The lattice has one job: surface the single most legitimate next move, with full reasoning for why, and a clear account of what happens if you do nothing.

## II. Life Domains (The Arenas)

These are the top-level containers. Everything scored in the lattice belongs to at least one domain. Domains do not compete directly — they inform which comparator is active.

| Domain | Description |
|---|---|
| **ARCHITECTURE** | Building systems, frameworks, theories, tools — intellectual construction work |
| **BUSINESS** | Revenue, clients, operations, Comeketo and downstream ventures |
| **HEALTH** | Physical capacity, energy management, body maintenance, sobriety architecture |
| **RELATIONSHIPS** | People who matter — loyalty, presence, repair, investment |
| **MASTERY** | Skill acquisition, learning, domains being entered and driven toward competence |
| **FINANCIAL** | Wealth building, leverage, financial stability, assets that compound |
| **IDENTITY** | The self-narrative, values in practice, who you are becoming under pressure |

These domains are not weighted equally. The lattice does not pretend they are. Their relative weight is declared in the scoring profile and can shift as your life phase shifts.

## III. The 8 Personal Base Scores

Derived from the JakeRL cognitive profile (309 Q&A pairs, 15 trait clusters by prevalence). These are not generic productivity scores. They are Jake-specific dimensions — the actual axes along which you evaluate everything, whether you know it or not.

### 1. CLOSURE POTENTIAL `(0–100)`
*Derived from: GROUNDING_INSTINCT (24.6%) + CLOSURE_LEGITIMACY (19.4%) — the two highest-prevalence traits in the entire JakeRL corpus.*

Can this thing actually close? Does it have a real finish line that cashes out against reality — not just a seductive direction? High score = clear closure conditions exist and are achievable. Low score = this is open-ended motion with no declared terminus.

**Oracle question:** *Has this earned the right to be treated as closable?*

---

### 2. COUNTERFEIT RISK `(0–100)`
*Derived from: ANTI_COUNTERFEIT_METRICS (8.1%) + biographical material on self-deception through motion.*

Inverted score — higher means more risk that this is fake progress. Is this producing real advancement or just activity that records itself as advancement? Jake's deepest allergy is expensive motion that doesn't cash out. This score catches it before it metastasizes.

**Oracle question:** *Is this real movement or counterfeit movement?*

---

### 3. COMPOUNDING LEVERAGE `(0–100)`
*Derived from: COMPOUNDING_EFFORT (10.0%) + ORCHESTRATION_OVER_MONOLITH (10.4%)*

Does doing this thing build future capacity — or just consume current capacity? High score = this action becomes infrastructure, creates reusable assets, or enables multiple future moves. Low score = one-time transaction with no downstream compound effect.

**Oracle question:** *Does finishing this make the next ten things easier?*

---

### 4. COGNITIVE COST `(0–100)`
*Derived from: BOUNDEDNESS (17.2%) — the third highest prevalence trait.*

Inverted score — higher means more expensive. What does this demand from Jake's finite cognitive and emotional capacity? Treats capacity as a real constraint, not an afterthought. The oracle refuses to recommend high-cost actions when the capacity envelope is already full.

**Oracle question:** *What does this actually cost to hold and execute?*

---

### 5. MOMENTUM ALIGNMENT `(0–100)`
*Derived from: REGIME_AWARENESS (16.5%) + EXPANSION_THEN_COMPRESSION (8.1%) + TEMPORAL_HIERARCHY (7.8%)*

Is this the right move for the current phase? Jake operates in phases — expansion, compression, stabilization, adversarial pressure. A move that's excellent in expansion phase is often wrong in compression phase. This score measures fit between the action and the current operating regime.

**Oracle question:** *Is this the right kind of move for where I actually am right now?*

---

### 6. STRUCTURAL CLARITY `(0–100)`
*Derived from: INSPECTABILITY (12.3%) + NAMING_POWER (7.1%)*

Can Jake see what's actually happening with this? High score = the situation is well-named, well-bounded, explicitly declared. Low score = opaque, fuzzy, or self-referential. The oracle cannot recommend action on structurally unclear objects — naming is a prerequisite for scoring.

**Oracle question:** *Do I actually know what this is?*

---

### 7. IDENTITY INTEGRITY `(0–100)`
*Derived from: CONSEQUENCE_ETHICS (7.1%) + RELATION_FIRST (15.9%) + biographical sections on self-narrative and defectiveness/legitimacy axis.*

Does this serve who Jake is building himself to be? Not who he was — who he is becoming. This score is the lattice's resistance to drift. High score = this action reinforces the self-narrative and the person under construction. Low score = this action is disconnected from or in tension with core identity.

**Oracle question:** *Is this consistent with the person I am claiming to be?*

---

### 8. RELATIONAL CONSEQUENCE `(0–100)`
*Derived from: RELATION_FIRST (15.9%) + biographical material on loyalty, mobilization under pressure, people who matter.*

What does this do to the people and relationships that matter? Jake mobilizes fiercely under conditions of real love and real stakes. This score ensures the oracle accounts for relational weight — not as soft sentiment but as structural consequence. High score = this action honors or strengthens what matters relationally. Low score = this action creates relational debt or damage.

**Oracle question:** *What does this do to the people I'm responsible to?*

## V. The Comparator System

The comparator is the declared basis for ranking. Without a declared comparator, there is no ranking — only a pile. The lattice supports these comparators out of the gate:

| Comparator | Reorders queue by... |
|---|---|
| `action_now` | What should I do in the next 2 hours |
| `highest_leverage` | What will compound most over the next 90 days |
| `lowest_counterfeit_risk` | What is the most real and grounded right now |
| `domain:BUSINESS` | What matters most in the business domain specifically |
| `domain:HEALTH` | What matters most in the health domain |
| `domain:IDENTITY` | What is most aligned with who I'm becoming |
| `energy_match:low` | What can I execute at low energy without counterfeit risk |
| `energy_match:high` | What deserves my best cognitive hours |

The active comparator is declared at the start of every oracle session. Change it and the entire queue reshuffles. Same objects — different ranking law.

## VI. Signal Types (The Intake Layer)

Signals are the events that cause the oracle to re-score. They are not entered manually as tasks. They come in from wherever Jake's life actually produces information.

### Signal Source Roles

| Source | Role | What the oracle does with it |
|---|---|---|
| **Slack** | Primary operational feed | Reads what's being discussed; surfaces items where Jake can take meaningful action; does NOT treat every message as a task |
| **iMessage** | Relationship + commitment signal | Reads incoming threads for commitments made, decisions pending, relationship signals — subject to blacklist rules (see Section X) |
| **ClickUp** | Project + task state | Tracks what's stalled, what's in motion, what's overdue; feeds Momentum Alignment scoring |
| **Calendar** | Time constraint + output layer | Reads upcoming deadlines and blocked time; oracle also *writes* to calendar — if it identifies a commitment or deadline, it proposes a calendar entry |
| **Close CRM** | Business domain only | Pipeline events feed the BUSINESS domain score; kept separate from personal domains |
| **Manual intake** | Override and declaration | Jake directly tells the oracle about a decision, new item, or energy state |

### Important: Oracle's relationship to Slack
Jake's job is not to manage the CRM. Jake's job is to see what's happening in Slack and determine if there's something worth acting on. The oracle reads Slack as an observer — it flags threads where action by Jake has meaningful impact, ignores noise, and surfaces a clear "here's what you could do and why it matters" rather than a firehose.

### Important: Calendar as output
Jake does not currently maintain a calendar. The oracle will begin building one from signals — if a commitment, deadline, or time-sensitive item appears in any signal source, the oracle proposes a calendar event. Over time the calendar becomes a real-time reflection of what the lattice knows about Jake's life.

### Signal Event Types:
| Signal | What it means for scoring |
|---|---|
| `weekly_knowledge_sweep` | Web/YouTube sweep for AI industry news (→ team via #ai-topics) and physics/math (→ personal research). Two audiences, two feeds, one sweep. |
| `slack_action_opportunity` | Oracle detects a Slack thread where Jake's involvement would matter |
| `imessage_commitment_detected` | A message thread contains an implied or explicit commitment |
| `new_commitment_declared` | New item enters the lattice; scores assigned |
| `deadline_approaching` | Closure Potential and Action Now scores increase |
| `calendar_event_proposed` | Oracle identifies a time-sensitive item and proposes a calendar entry |
| `stalled_item_detected` | Counterfeit Risk score increases; oracle flags |
| `domain_phase_shift` | Current operating regime changes; Momentum Alignment recalculates |
| `identity_tension_flagged` | Jake or oracle detects misalignment; Identity Integrity drops |
| `energy_state_declared` | Jake declares high/low/depleted; active comparator adjusts |
| `completion_logged` | Item closes; scoring model updates; compounding effect recorded |

Every oracle output follows this structure — same as the CRM oracle, adapted for life:

```
SINGLE NEXT MOVE
─────────────────────────────────────
Action: [Exact thing to do — verb + object + context]
Domain: [Which life domain]
Comparator active: [What basis is being used to rank this]

WHY THIS IS THE MOVE
[2–3 sentences: why this beats every other item in the queue right now]

IF YOU DO NOTHING TODAY
[1–2 sentences: what actually happens if this sits — concrete consequence, not vague warning]

WHAT SUPPORTS THIS
- [Signal or score that pushed this to the top]
- [Secondary supporting factor]

WHAT ARGUES AGAINST
- [Honest blocking signal or cost]

APPROVE / REVISE / REJECT
[After action: log the outcome and grade the recommendation]
```

## VIII. Canonical Rules (Lattice Invariants)

These cannot be overridden by any scoring profile or comparator:

1. **Nothing scores in isolation.** Every item is ranked relative to everything else in the active queue.

2. **Counterfeit progress is disqualifying.** An item with Counterfeit Risk above 75 cannot be the top recommendation regardless of other scores.

3. **Structural Clarity is a prerequisite.** An item scoring below 30 on Structural Clarity is not scoreable — it must be named and bounded before it enters the ranking.

4. **The oracle does not moralize.** It ranks. The human decides. The human's approval or rejection is itself a signal that adjusts future recommendations.

5. **Every recommendation must have a counterfactual.** If the oracle cannot state what happens if the action is not taken, the recommendation is not legitimate.

6. **Scoring profiles are versioned.** When weights change, the version increments. History is immutable — the rationale for past recommendations does not get retroactively rewritten.

7. **Energy state is declared, not inferred.** The oracle never assumes Jake has capacity. If no energy state is declared, it defaults to `energy_match:moderate`.
